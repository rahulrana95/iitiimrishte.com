# Stage 1 — Getting In
_You want to see if you're good enough to get in — and then actually get in._

**Covers:** ONB-1, ONB-3, ONB-4 (and the invite/vouch path)

## The journey (plain language, numbered)
1. You land on the page. There's no "Sign up" button — only **"Apply for an invitation."** The scarcity is the point: this is a room you get *let into*, not one you walk into.
2. You fill a short application: email or Google/Apple/LinkedIn, your gender, your city/corridor, and what makes you eligible (your institute, or your employer + title).
3. You verify you're a real, eligible person — government ID + a quick selfie, plus at least one credential (degree or job). **This happens before any queue**, so everyone waiting is already pre-qualified.
4. Now the two paths split — same verification for everyone, different *pace*:
   - **If you're a woman:** you're in. Instantly. No queue, no fee. Straight to building your profile.
   - **If you're a man:** you join a visible waitlist with a real position number. Men are admitted in balanced weekly batches, sized to how many active women are actually there — so replies stay real.
5. While you wait (men), you can **move up honestly**: refer a verified qualified peer, add more verification badges, or get vouched by a member. No paying to skip the line.
6. When your batch is called, you're **Admitted** — you build your profile and become discoverable.
7. Special path: if a member invited or vouched for you, your invite link carries you straight into the funnel (and can speed up admission), with a "Vouched by a verified member" badge waiting on your profile.
