# Team Role: Brand, PR & Positioning

_Raw agent output (condensed)._

**Strategic problem:** incumbent proved demand then poisoned the category with elitism/segregation/sexism + founder-credibility gap. Own what they got right (rigor, verification, seriousness), be structurally immune to the backlash. **"We don't sell status. We sell certainty."**

## Positioning & name
*shaadi* (transaction/endpoint) → *rishta* (relationship/bond) = the whole strategy in one word; warmer, diaspora-inclusive, translates as "the connection." "iitiim" prefix is a **liability to reframe, not lean on** — recall equity, but never the hero of public creative; the hero is always verification + seriousness. Positioning: *the most trusted place in the world for accomplished, serious people to meet — verified, private, international.* Premium = **high-signal & verified, never high-status**. (Flagged to leadership: prefix invites "just the elite club again?" in every press hit — keep the domain, never make IIT/IIM the hero.)

## Taglines
Master: **"Everyone here is real. Everyone here is serious."** Acquisition line: **"Meet someone equally serious."** Others: "Verified people. Real intentions." / "For people who found everything except the person." / "Accomplished, and looking for the same."

## Core story (emphasis order)
1. Trust is the product. 2. Seriousness over status. 3. Achievement has many paths (surgeon/founder/researcher/operator/alumnus). 4. Private by design, international by default.

### "Premium" without "elitist" (swap identity words → standard words)
Elite/exclusive → Verified/serious/high-signal · "only IIT/IIM" → "accomplished, however they got there" · "the best people" → "people who are actually serious" · "a class apart" → "everyone here has been verified". **Elitism = a claim others are lesser; standards = what we require of everyone equally.**

## Messaging pillars (share of voice)
VERIFIED 40% · SERIOUS 25% · RESPECTFUL & FAIR 20% · PRIVATE & GLOBAL 15%. Tone: confident not superior, warm not saccharine, plain not jargon, adult/self-aware, never punch down.

### Talking to women vs men (the tightrope — incumbent attacked from BOTH sides)
Single fairness narrative, mirrored emphases. Unifying frame: *"We built this so a real, serious message actually gets a fair reading — on both sides."* To women: safety, control, respect ("you decide who gets your attention"; never scarce-resource/decorative framing). To men: fairness, signal, a real shot ("write something real to someone real, it gets a fair reading"; product-proof not whining). **Rule: the same sentence should be true and welcome to both genders — the "screenshot side-by-side" test.**

## Anti-elitism strategy
Do's: verification/safety as headline; premium = verified achievement many paths; identical standards all genders visibly; center user not founder pedigree; talk who we welcome not who we reject; openly name the category's baggage.
Don'ts (each → an incumbent wound): don't rank institutes/people (Homegrown/Blind); don't describe women by looks/family/"eligible" (Feminism in India 2014); no segregationist "keep our kind together" (KJo); never caste (optional buried preference only); don't build brand on celebrity/founder degree (KJo + "dogalapan" troll); no unaudited vanity metrics (5-lakh-vs-30k); never mock those "below the bar."
Proactive inoculation: publish a plain-language **"Our Standards, and Why"** page at launch (verify everyone equally, don't rank, no caste, broad accomplishment, "verified ≠ vouched").

## Launch PR
- **Founding story = trust-origin, not pedigree-origin** (built the trust infra we wished existed; not "I'm elite too" → sidesteps dogalapan). Founder authority = builder's credibility (trust/safety/verification depth), never member-eligibility.
- Credibility signals: readable verification methodology; published privacy/data-safety posture; honest metrics (active/verified); third-party pen-test + bug-bounty + named DPO; real consented outcomes.
- Thought leadership in the **critique-adjacent outlets** (Homegrown/Quartz/feminist-tech-policy audiences primed for a credible reformer): "why elite matchmaking deserved its backlash & what fixing it requires," "verification vs vetting," "the fairness problem," "protecting the most sensitive data."
- **Celebrity: HARD NO marquee ambassador at launch (KJo lesson — fame amplifies existing cracks).** Use credibility voices (trust/safety experts, psychologists), real members as heroes, niche micro-influencers (diaspora/women-in-tech). Vet every endorser on the four fault lines (nepotism/elitism/sexism/inauthenticity); "screenshot next to anti-elitism page" test.
- Launch sequence: waitlist (scarcity framed as *rigor* not snobbery) → founding-story + methodology drop → thought-leadership wave → proof phase (transparency report + consented stories) → quarterly transparency reports forever.

## Trust-signal strategy (trust = brand)
Visible legible verification badges w/ provenance; sell honesty of limits ("verified ≠ vouched" as a trust flex); symmetric verification as fairness signal; privacy as headline product (non-enumerable IDs, incognito, consent-gated photos, isolated vault, erasure); real outcomes over vanity metrics forever (recurring Transparency Report competitors can't copy without exposing their padding); safety made felt.

## 3 campaign concepts
1. **"Real. Serious. Verified."** (launch/brand) — anti-ghosting/fakes split-frame; diverse "accomplished" casting; premium signal = verified, never elite.
2. **"A Fair Reply"** (fairness/gender) — two mirrored films (woman on her terms / man's real message gets read), same closing line, designed for screenshot-side-by-side; inoculates Feminism-in-India + Quartz at once.
3. **"What 'Verified' Actually Means"** (trust/thought-leadership) — transparency-as-marketing microsite walking through verification + privacy + "verified ≠ vouched" + first Transparency Report.

**Governing sentence:** we don't sell status — we sell certainty.
